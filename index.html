Dengue Fever Overview

Dengue is a viral illness caused by four related dengue viruses. It's spread through Aedes mosquito bites and commonly found in tropical and subtropical areas.

Transmission & Breeding

Transmitted by Aedes aegypti mosquitoes. They breed in stagnant water like flower pots, buckets, and old tires. Awareness is crucial to stop breeding sources.

Symptoms

Symptoms include high fever, headache, eye pain, joint/muscle pain, vomiting, and rash. It appears 3 to 14 days after being bitten.

Treatment Options

No antiviral treatment exists. Rest, fluids, and paracetamol are recommended. Avoid NSAIDs. In serious cases, blood transfusion may be needed.

Prevention Tips

Use mosquito nets, wear full-sleeve clothing, apply repellents, and destroy mosquito habitats. Prevention is the best protection.

Community Awareness

Rainy season increases risk. Stay alert and involved. Community efforts like cleaning water sources and reporting outbreaks help control dengue.
